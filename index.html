Power & Privilege: The Socio-Economic Pattern of Solar

Key Takeaways
  • 2009Solar Credits arrive; solar water heater peaks and PV momentum begins.
  • PV dominates the later years while solar water heater tapers off.
  • ~2018-2021 A decade on, both technologies surge again as costs fall.
Top contributors to total installations
New South Wales Queensland Victoria
  • Hotter and sunnier states are likely to adopt Solar Photovoltaic Installations.
  • Whereas, colder states demand more on Solar Water Heater Installations.
  • Socio-Economic Indexes for Areas (SEIFA), SEIFA combines Census data such as income, education, employment, occupation, housing and family structure to summarise the socio-economic characteristics of an area.
  • Australian Capital Territory has the highest SEIFA score, next comes New South Wales and Victoria.
  • Solar PVStrong positive relationship between SEIFA score and the number of installations in NSW and VIC
  • Solar Water HeaterStrong positive relationship is also observed in those states
  • Exceptionally, ACT has low number of installations in both types
  • SEI helps, but isn’t decisive: Wealthier states adopt more, yet policy, housing type, and population size are major co-drivers.
