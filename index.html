Power & Privilege: The Socio-Economic Pattern of Solar

How Australia's solar adoption evolved since 2001-by technology, by state, and in relation to socio-economic advantage.

Key Takeaways

  • PV dominates the later years while solar water heater tapers off.

Socio-Economic Index & Solar Uptake

SEIFA
  • SEIFA combines Census indicators—income, education, employment, occupation, housing, and family structure— to summarise the socio-economic characteristics of an area.
  • ACT has the highest SEIFA score, followed by NSW and VIC.
  • Solar PV Strong positive relationship between SEIFA and installations in NSW and VIC.
  • Solar Water Heater Similar positive relationship observed in those states.
  • Exception: ACT shows relatively low installations for both technologies despite high SEIFA.
  • SEIFA helps but isn’t decisive: wealth, policy settings, housing type, and population size all co-drive adoption.
